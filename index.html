About

Expert in sustainable architecture, across typologies and scales.

Remon Alberts, AIA, CPHD, LEED AP BD+C is a Senior Associate at COOKFOX Architects, where he has played a key role in advancing the firm's mission-driven design approach since 2021. He brings deep experience across multifamily residential and institutional work, currently leading design management for 65 Franklin, a residential tower on Broadway in Lower Manhattan.

Prior to COOKFOX, Remon worked at Thomas Phifer and Partners, contributing to cultural projects including a new wing for the Corning Museum of Glass and the Museum of Modern Art in Warsaw. Earlier, he was part of the team at NADAAA in Boston, working on Rock Creek House, a single-family residence in Washington, D.C.

Remon holds a degree in architecture from Delft University of Technology, where he graduated with honors. He brings a rigorous interest in comparative urbanism and energy policy to both design and advocacy — serving as a certified Passive House designer, AIA COTE member, contributor to Architecture 2030's reporting efforts, and visiting critic at universities in Boston and New York City.
